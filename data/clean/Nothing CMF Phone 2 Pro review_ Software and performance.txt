Nothing CMF Android
Nothing OS on top of Android 15
The CMF Phone 2 Pro embraces may sit under a sub-brand, but its UI is identical to the
Nothing phones. It runs on Nothing OS - specifically, version 3.2 layered over Android 15.
Nothing commits to delivering up to three major Android updates to the CMF Phone 2 Pro.
Nothing OS remains minimalist in terms of functionality and stays close to stock Android
(AOSP), but it carries a distinct visual identity. The UI is heavily styled with Nothing's
characteristic dot matrix font and design language. Users can access 24 custom widgets,
covering essentials like clocks, calendars, contacts, and weather, all designed to maintain a
cohesive aesthetic.

The notification shade and quick settings strike a balance between stock Android and
Nothing's customizations. Starting with Nothing OS 2.5, the top two large quick toggles
switched from circular to rounded-square designs, a change retained in version 3.2.
Another recent feature is the custom icon pack, which sticks to a uniform monochrome style,

Nothing OS 3.2
The CMF Phone 1 lacked always-on display support, but the Phone 2 Pro rectifies this. It is as
basic as they come, though, with only a clock and a few notification icons, and no
customization options.
Always On display
Nothing OS includes a Monochrome UI mode for users seeking an even more distinctive
interface. While it ties into features like Do Not Disturb and Bedtime, it can be configured
independently. Importantly, this is a visual effect only - screenshots and photos will still appear
in full color.
The Nothing X app provides seamless integration with several audio products, including the
Ear (1), Ear (stick), Ear (2), CMF Buds, Neckband Pro, and Buds Pro.

The CMF Phone 2 Pro is refreshingly clean of bloatware, provided you don't count the
standard suite of Google apps. The only pre-installed apps from Nothing include Camera,
Weather, and Recorder, all featuring a unified design. The Nothing X app was also preloaded
on our review unit, but it can be downloaded from the Play Store if it's missing.
Benchmarks and performance
A Dimensity 7300 Pro chipset powers the Pro phone. It's a variant of the silicon that powered
the first CMF Phone, but now with a 10% faster CPU and 5% faster GPU. The MediaTek
Dimensity 7300 Pro is a 4nm, pretty modern part.
It has four Cortex-A78 cores clocked at up to 2.5 GHz, and another four Cortex-A55 cores,
working at up to 2.0 GHz. The onboard GPU is a Mali-G615 MC2. The CMF Phone 2 Pro
comes with 8GB of RAM; you can get it with 128GB or 256GB of built-in storage that can be
further expanded via the microSD slot. Our speed testing indicates that the phone uses UFS
2.2 or perhaps 3.0 chips. Our review unit is the 8GB/256GB one.
Let's start with CPU benchmarks and Geekbench first. The burst performance of the
Dimensity 7300 Pro inside the CMF Phone 2 Pro is similar to that of the regular Dimensity
7300 inside the CMF Phone 1. That's solid for this price range. Still, if you are after slightly
bigger numbers, you can also find a Snapdragon 7s Gen 3 inside, the Redmi Note 14 Pro+

Thermal-throttling
The CMF Phone 2 Pro handles heat very well. We put it through its paces and subjected it to
quite a bit of torture testing. Even so, it managed to maintain a large chunk of its performance
without exhibiting sudden and jarring dips.
