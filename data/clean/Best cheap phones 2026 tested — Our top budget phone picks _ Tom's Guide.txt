The iPhone 17e is the refined version of the iPhone 16e that Apple
should have given us from the beginning. While sticking at the
predecessor by finally integrating MagSafe connectivity, doubling
the starting storage to 256GB, and adopting a more powerful A19
chip. While it retains the same overall design as the 16e, the
inclusion of MagSafe magnets for accessories and wireless
charging makes the 17e a more complete "budget" option than
last year's somewhat compromised model.
The iPhone 17e sticks with Apple's traditional display notch and a
60Hz refresh rate, making for a less-than-favorable comparison
with the other phones on this list. Fortunately, the iPhone 17e has
gained Ceramic Shield 2, a material shared with the more
expensive iPhone 17 that promises significantly better scratch
resistance than older models.
The 17e remains limited to a single rear camera lens—though it
Best cheap phones 2026 tested — Our top budget phone pick...
does leverage software for "optical-quality" 2x crops and
improved portrait effects. Other phones on this list offer at least
an ultrawide camera on their backs, which gives extra versatility
even if their image processing isn't on par with Apple's.
The iPhone 17e is the strongest value play for anyone currently
using an iPhone 14, or an older SE model. By bridging the gap
between the budget and flagship tiers, it is a compelling option
for those who want the reliability and performance of the latest
iOS ecosystem without the extra cost of a secondary camera lens
or the high-refresh-rate display of the flagship iPhone 17.

The Samsung Galaxy A57 stands out for its expansive 6.7-inch
AMOLED display, flagship-grade materials and impressive battery
endurance, despite being a budget phone. This combination
makes it appealing for anyone who prioritizes reliability over
outright performance.
Best cheap phones 2026 tested — Our top budget phone pick...
The Galaxy A57 excels in its integration with Samsung's
ecosystem, including the latest Galaxy AI features. Tools like live
translation and AI photo editing are available, providing a taste of
Samsung’s flagship-level software experience at a more
accessible price point. For buyers invested in Samsung’s
ecosystem or those who value timely updates and device
interconnection, the A57 offers strong long-term value.
On the camera front, the A57 features a triple-lens setup: a 50MP
main sensor, a 12MP ultrawide, and a 5MP macro lens. Setting the
disappointing macro aside, the A57's cameras deliver versatility
for everyday shooting, keeping pace with what most buyers
expect in the midrange Samsung line-up.
One area where the A57 shows its budget roots is charging
to the fastest-charging rivals, which can be a minor frustration for
heavy users or anyone rushing out the door.
Stacked against the Pixel 10a and Nothing Phone 4a Pro, the
A57’s larger screen and superior battery life are clear advantages.
It lags behind those competitors in AI-powered photography,
peak display brightness, and raw processing power, so
performance-focused users may notice the difference in
demanding apps or outdoor use. Plus, when you see how little
changed from last year's Galaxy A56, while Samsung increased
the price, takes the shine off of the other elements somewhat.
The Galaxy A57 is best suited for Samsung loyalists and value-
seekers who care most about display size, reliable battery life,
and a familiar camera experience. If you want a taste of flagship
software and don’t mind the trade-offs in charging and
performance, it deserves a close look.
