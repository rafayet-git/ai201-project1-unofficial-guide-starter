I'm currently using a 9a, I got mine as a birthday gift which is why I'm using it as a secondary
phone. But there's a reason why it's my secondary and not my primary, it stinks
I know you've probably heard this thousands of times, but it's true; pixel phones have awful
performance compared to its competitors Apps lag out after a while. I've experienced some
UI glitches too. I had to get this phone replaced once because my first one (the one I bought
before my birthday) had a connectivity issue. Speaking about the connectivity, the modem on
this phone is absolutely awful. It has a trash Exynos 5300 modem. Although the modem is an
improvement compared to the ones used on the Pixel 7 series, my data speeds are still much
lower on my Pixel 9a in comparison to my other phones, I used the same SIM for all of them to
test the speeds out. The charging is also snail slow, it took me 1h 37mins just to charge me
phone from 5% to 90%. Lastly, there's this strange popping noise that ocurrs every once in a
while, even if your volume is on mute. For me this popping sound happens when I'm on
YouTube or when I'm opening a game. This popping sound is an issue for all Pixel phones in
general, but some get this popping sound more than others.
But there are many things that I love about the Pixel 9a. I love the Iris color on mine, there is no
camera bump, the bezels aren't that noticeable, if you buy the phone via a carrier there won't
be any carrier bloat, you'll be the first to receive new updates, the camera is great (but the
zoom is very limited; you can only use up to 8x zoom on photo mode, and 4k 60fps videos can
only go up to 5x zoom). The UI is gorgeous, it's very customizable, and the haptics feel great
